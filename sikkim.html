Sikkim
Embraced by the mighty Himalayas, Sikkim enchants travelers yearning for a haven of tranquility. Its breathtaking tapestry of landscapes, from snow-kissed peaks to emerald valleys, unfolds a serene escape, cleansing away the burdens of city life and restoring your spirit with newfound peace and rejuvenation.

Pebbles on the water

Sikkim's majestic mountainscapes, from the snow-capped peak of Kanchenjunga to the vibrant tapestry of Yumthang Valley, offer an unforgettable adventure unlike any other. Imagine trekking through alpine meadows bursting with wildflowers, the crisp mountain air filling your lungs as you admire the endless beauty of the Himalayas. The majestic Kanchenjunga, towering over the horizon, will leave you breathless, while the vibrant blooms of Yumthang Valley, known as the Valley of Flowers, paint a mesmerizing landscape. Whether you're a nature enthusiast seeking breathtaking views or simply someone looking for a unique way to experience Sikkim, these attractions promise a journey that's both exhilarating and awe-inspiring.

Let's Go Back in Time

  • Early Inhabitants: Sikkim's history begins with the indigenous Lepcha people, believed to be the first inhabitants. They were followed by Bhutia migrants from Tibet in the 14th century, laying the foundation for a cultural blend.
  • Rise of the Namgyals: The 17th century marked a turning point with the establishment of the Namgyal dynasty. These Buddhist priest-kings, known as Chogyals, ruled Sikkim for centuries, shaping its political and spiritual landscape.
  • A Himalayan Kingdom: Sikkim thrived as a Himalayan kingdom, facing challenges from its neighbors. Territorial disputes with Bhutan and Nepal arose, with the British Empire emerging as a key player in the region.
  • British Protectorate: By the 19th century, Sikkim became a British protectorate. The strategically important Darjeeling was ceded to the British, influencing Sikkim's future course.
  • Post-Independence Status: After India's independence in 1947, Sikkim retained its protectorate status with the new Indian government. It enjoyed a period of relative autonomy with a high literacy rate.
  • Merger with India: The 1970s saw a period of political unrest, culminating in the overthrow of the Chogyal monarchy in 1975. Sikkim's status transitioned into a full-fledged state of India through a controversial referendum.
