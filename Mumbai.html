Mumbai
Ditch the Bollywood glitz and forget the fancy high-rises for a sec! Mumbai has way more to offer than just bright lights and movie stars. Dive into the city's colonial past and imagine merchants and monsoons! Mumbai was once a group of islands called Bombay, a trading center for the British Empire. Explore the grand colonial buildings and imagine the bustling port filled with ships carrying exotic goods from all over the world.

Bandra-Worli Sea Link

Mumbai's bridges, the Bandra-Worli Sea Link and the new Trans Harbour bridge, soar across the cityscape, offering a breathtaking perspective unlike any other. Imagine cruising along these engineering marvels, the wind whipping through your hair as you admire the endless expanse of the Arabian Sea. The iconic Bandra-Worli Sea Link stretches out like a majestic necklace, while the Trans Harbour Bridge, India's longest, unveils a panoramic vista of the city's vibrant skyline. Whether you're a photography enthusiast capturing stunning cityscapes or simply someone seeking a unique way to experience Mumbai, these bridges promise a journey that's both exhilarating and awe-inspiring.

Let's Go Back in Time

  • Island Beginnings: Mumbai's story starts on a cluster of seven islands, once home to the Koli fishing community. Archaeologists believe people lived here for millennia, but by 1000 BCE, the islands were a thriving trade center, linked to Egypt and Persia.
  • Kingdoms and Empires: Over the centuries, Mumbai saw various rulers. The Mauryan Empire under Ashoka the Great left its mark, followed by the Silhara dynasty and Gujarat Sultanate. Each era added its own layer to the city's heritage.
  • The Portuguese Arrival: In 1534, the Portuguese entered the scene. They renamed the islands Bombay and ruled for over 200 years. This period saw the construction of magnificent forts and churches, shaping Mumbai's architectural landscape.
  • A British Boom: In 1661, Bombay became British as part of a royal dowry. The British Raj transformed the city. They merged the islands, built grand buildings like the Gateway of India, and established Mumbai as a major trading port, shaping it into the financial powerhouse it is today.
  • Fight for Freedom: Mumbai played a crucial role in India's independence movement. The city witnessed numerous protests and rallies, with leaders like Lokmanya Tilak emerging from here. Finally, in 1947, India achieved freedom, and Bombay became a part of the newly formed nation.
  • Mumbai Today: Modern Mumbai is a bustling metropolis, a melting pot of cultures and religions. It's India's financial and entertainment capital, famous for Bollywood, its street food scene, and iconic landmarks. Despite its growth, traces of its rich past are still visible, waiting to be explored.
