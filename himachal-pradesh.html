Himachal Pradesh's enchanting landscapes, from the snow-capped peaks of the Himalayas to the lush valleys of Kullu and Manali, offer an unforgettable adventure unlike any other. Imagine trekking through valleys carpeted with wildflowers, the crisp mountain air invigorating you as you admire the endless beauty of the Himalayas. Towering peaks pierce the clouds, while serene valleys like Kullu and Manali offer a haven of tranquility. Whether you're a thrill-seeker scaling icy slopes or simply someone seeking a peaceful escape amidst nature's grandeur, Himachal Pradesh's attractions promise a journey that's both exhilarating and soul-stirring.

Let's Go Back in Time

  • Ancient Republics: Himachal Pradesh's history boasts a rich past, with evidence suggesting the presence of numerous small republics as early as the Mahabharata era. These Janapadas, like the Audumbras and Kulindas, flourished in the lower and upper hills, laying the foundation for a distinct way of life.
  • Empires and Kingdoms: Over time, Himachal Pradesh witnessed the rise and fall of empires and kingdoms. The Mauryan Empire under Chandragupta Maurya extended its influence in the 3rd century BCE, followed by the Gupta Empire's reach in the 4th century CE. Powerful regional kingdoms like Chamba, Kangra, and Kullu also emerged, leaving behind a legacy of art, architecture, and hill forts.
  • Medieval Period: The medieval period saw the influence of Hinduism and Buddhism flourish in Himachal. The region became a مرکز (markaz, center) for religious scholarship, with monasteries and temples like Baijnathpur attracting pilgrims. Trade routes connecting Tibet and Central Asia also passed through Himachal, boosting its cultural and economic significance.
  • Mughal Influence: The Mughal Empire extended its reach into the foothills of Himachal Pradesh in the 16th century. While some areas remained largely autonomous, the Mughals' influence is evident in forts and administrative structures.
  • Colonial Era: The 18th and 19th centuries saw the rise of the British East India Company. Following the Gurkha War (1815-1816), the British annexed most of Himachal Pradesh, integrating it into British India. The colonial period saw the introduction of a new administrative system and the development of hill stations like Shimla, which became the British summer capital.
  • Post-Independence Journey: After India's independence in 1947, several princely states and chiefdoms in the region were merged to form Himachal Pradesh. It gained the status of a Union Territory in 1956 and finally achieved statehood in 1971. Since then, Himachal Pradesh has emerged as a leading tourist destination while focusing on preserving its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty.

Itinerary

Day 1:

Shimla arrival
Former summer capital of British India & now a capital of Himachal Pradesh set amidst the snow-capped shivalik mountain ranges offering some stunning views of the mighty Himalayas; it is surrounded by pine deodar, oak and rhododendron forests. Shimla offers a variety of shopping, sport and entertainment activities.

Day 2:

Shimla - Kufri - Shimla
Today morning after breakfast visit the Kufri a 30 Minutes’ drive from Shimla, an exotica of nature as a place surrounded by dense forest and snow caped mountains amidst virgin peaks and natures flora and fauna where one can completely relax and tune oneself for natural beauty, situated at an altitude of 2500 meters and a very popular ski resort during the winter months. Later take a walking tour of Shimla &, visit the Jakhoo hills which, offers panoramic views of the Shimla town, “Vice-regal lodge” “Mall road” ”Lakkar Bazaar” &“Ridge” later in the evening back to the hotel Overnight stay at Hotel in Shimla

Day 3:

Shimla - Manali
Manali: at the northern end of the Kullu Valley in Himachal Pradesh, is a hill station situated at a height of 2050 m (6398 ft.) in the Himalayas. It is a popular tourist spot for Indians in summer and a magical, snow-covered place in winter. A staging point for a number of treks and sports such as white-water rafting, Rohtang pass which is main attraction near Manali Arrive at Manali and check in the hotel, Rest of the day is at leisure to visit the local markets. Overnight stay at hotel in Manali

Day 4:

Manali
After breakfast visit to city tour. Visiting “Hadimba temple”, the 450 years old Hadimba Devi Temple, the oldest temple in Manali, “Vashisht Temple” “Van Vihar” “Club House” Tibetan market & Mall Road. Evening back to Hotel Overnight stay at hotel in Manali

Day 5:

Manali – Chandigarh
After breakfast check out from hotel, drive to Chandigarh Later visit to Rock Garden, Rose Garden and Sukhna Lake (Depending upon the time) Overnight stay at hotel in Chandigarh

Day 6:

Chandigarh Departure
Breakfast & check out from the hotel. Later transfer to airport to catch the flight back to your destination.
