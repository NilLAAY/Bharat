Saputara
Nestled amidst the rolling hills of Gujarat, Saputara beckons as a haven of cool breezes and serene beauty. Crowned as the ‘Hill Station of Gujarat,’ it rests at a refreshing 1000 meters above sea level, offering a welcome respite from the scorching Indian plains. This ‘Abode of Serpents’ – as its name translates to – boasts a rich tapestry of history and nature waiting to be explored.

waterfalls

Cruise through paradise! Picture this: lush green hills in Saputara with waterfalls cascading down like nature's water slides. Hike through refreshing forests and feel the cool mist tickle your face. Sunshine dances on the sprays, creating mini rainbows all around. The star of the show? Gira Falls, a mighty 100-foot drop into a pool so refreshing, you'll want to jump right in! Whether you crave the power of Kaka Math Falls or the chill vibes of Hathikund, Saputara's waterfalls promise an epic adventure. Escape the crowds, reconnect with nature, and get ready for memories that splash!

Let's Go Back in Time

  • Hill Station History: Saputara's story stretches back centuries. Legends say it was a refuge for Lord Rama during his exile. The name itself, "Abode of Serpents," hints at a rich cultural past revered by the local tribes.
  • Kingdoms on the Hill: Over time, Saputara saw many rulers. From the powerful Mughal Empire to the Maratha Confederacy, it held strategic importance. Each era left its mark, adding to the region's unique tapestry.
  • Colonial Charm: In the 19th century, the British arrived, developing Saputara as a hill station to escape the scorching Indian summers. This period saw the construction of charming colonial bungalows and parks, adding a unique touch.
  • A Modern Escape: After India's independence, Saputara blossomed into a popular tourist destination. Today, it's known for its pleasant climate, stunning scenery, and adventure activities. Whether you're a history buff or a nature lover, Saputara offers a glimpse into the past nestled amidst breathtaking beauty.
