Ayodhaya
Outside the usual paths traveled by pilgrims, Ayodhya holds secrets waiting to be found. For adventurous explorers looking for a real adventure, Ayodhya, with its rich stories from ancient times and lively traditions, provides a special chance to explore India in a different way.

Celebration on reclaiming civilization

In the heart of Ayodhya sets a world record by lighting more lamps than anywhere else on Earth, transforming the city into a magical scene straight out of a storybook. Imagine the warm glow of millions of flickering flames reflecting on the Saryu River, creating a breathtaking atmosphere that celebrates the victory of good over evil and light over darkness. This incredible event is a must-see for anyone wanting to experience the vibrant culture and rich history of India, offering a glimpse into a tradition that's both beautiful and deeply meaningful.

Let's Go Back in Time

  • Ancient Origins: Believed to be one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, Ayodhya served as the capital of the ancient Kosala kingdom.
  • A Temple of Tradition: The construction of the original temple dedicated to Lord Rama in Ayodhya dates back to ancient times, cementing its significance as a sacred site for Hindus.
  • A Shift in Landscape: In 1528, the Babri Masjid was built by Mir Baqi, a general of Mughal Emperor Babur, at the site of the Ram Mandir. This marked a significant change in the city's religious landscape.
  • A Contested Legacy: The 20th century saw an intensification of the dispute over the ownership of the site, leading to conflicting claims from Hindu and Muslim communities.
  • A Tragic Turning Point: The demolition of the Babri Masjid in 1992 by Hindu nationalist groups sparked widespread communal violence, leaving a deep scar on the nation's history.
  • Seeking Resolution: Decades of legal battles ensued to determine the ownership of the disputed site.
  • A New Chapter: In November 2019, the Supreme Court of India ruled in favor of the construction of a Ram Mandir at the disputed site, while directing the government to allocate an alternative plot of land for the construction of a mosque.
  • Building Anew: Construction of the Ram Mandir began in August 2020, following the court verdict. Built according to traditional Hindu architectural principles, the temple is expected to become a symbol of cultural and religious pride, while also marking a new chapter in Ayodhya's long and storied history.
