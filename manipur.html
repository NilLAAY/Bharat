Manipur
Nestled in the gentle embrace of the Himalayas, Manipur offers a peaceful escape for weary travelers.Its breathtaking scenery, from majestic mountains and rolling hills to cascading waterfalls and serene valleys, provides a tranquil haven away from the hustle and bustle of city life.

House on water

Manipur's hidden gems, from the ethereal Loktak Lake adorned with phumdis (floating huts) to the serene Shirui Lily trek, offer an unforgettable adventure unlike any other. Imagine cruising across Loktak Lake, the world's only floating lake, on a traditional boat, the gentle sway of the water calming your mind as you admire the unique phumdis dotting the landscape. Hike through the rolling hills carpeted with the rare Shirui Lily, known as the "Dancing Flower," its vibrant colors painting a mesmerizing scene. Whether you're a nature enthusiast seeking off-the-beaten-path wonders or simply someone looking for a unique way to experience Manipur, these attractions promise a journey that's both tranquil and awe-inspiring.

Let's Go Back in Time

  • Ancient Origins: Manipur's history stretches back centuries, with legends tracing its lineage to the 15th century BCE. The Meitei people established a strong kingdom known as Kangleipak, laying the foundation for a distinct culture and civilization.
  • Shifting Alliances: Throughout its history, Manipur navigated a complex web of alliances. The kingdom faced threats from Burmese invasions in the 18th and 19th centuries, seeking support from the British at times.
  • The Burning of Scriptures: The 18th century also saw a unique event â€“ the legendary burning of sacred scriptures by King Pamheiba. This act aimed to establish a new religious code and unify the kingdom.
  • British Rule: Following the Anglo-Manipuri War in 1891, Manipur lost its independence and became a princely state under British rule. This period saw the introduction of new administrative structures and economic changes.
  • Merger with India: After India's independence in 1947, Manipur signed a Treaty of Accession to join the Indian Union. It became a Union Territory in 1956 and finally achieved full statehood in 1972.
  • A Legacy of Resilience: Despite facing challenges like insurgency and inter-ethnic conflicts, Manipur retains a rich cultural heritage. The state is known for its traditional dance forms, martial arts, and vibrant festivals, showcasing its enduring spirit.
